Born in Bragança Paulista and shaped by the reality of Vila Mota, Eduardo Adriano grew up hearing that he “would never be anyone.”
But he proved, with strength and purpose, that no one defines his path except himself.

He started working at 16 as a packer. Then came days as a stock clerk, warehouse assistant, shifts on the factory floor, and even loading trucks. Nothing came easy.

Today, Eduardo is a worldwide known entrepeneur and a powerful voice in promoting Black self-esteem. His story inspires because it breaks barriers, redefines narratives, and shows that Black identity is strength, beauty, and resilience.

A young Black man from the quebrada (slum) who believed in his own potential, in his own style, and turned it into a legacy.

Among his many trademarks, one immediately stands out: his braids. More than aesthetics, they carry history, ancestry, and self-esteem.

In the report “Pretas Ideias”, aired on Bom Dia SP and available on Globoplay, Eduardo Adriano appears alongside professionals like hairstylist Lourenço Menezes, highlighting the importance of braids—not just as a style, but as a symbol of ancestry, history, and empowerment.

The piece reinforces how reclaiming Black culture and identity transforms lives and opens paths for other Black people to also recognize themselves as protagonists of their own stories.
